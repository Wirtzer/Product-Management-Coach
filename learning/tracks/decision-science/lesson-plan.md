# Decision Science — Lesson Plan

**Updated:** 2026-01-01

## Current Status
New learner. No sessions completed yet. Begin with diagnostic to establish baseline.

## What to Cover First
1. **Cognitive biases in product decisions** — Learn the 5 biases most dangerous for PMs: confirmation bias, sunk cost fallacy, anchoring, availability heuristic, and survivorship bias. For each, identify a real product decision where it could lead you astray.
2. **Decision frameworks** — Learn structured approaches to decisions: weighted scoring, decision matrices, pre-mortem analysis, reversible vs. irreversible decisions. Understand when each is appropriate.
3. **Probabilistic thinking** — Practice thinking in probabilities rather than certainties. Learn to estimate likelihoods, understand expected value, and make decisions under uncertainty.

## Recommended First Exercise
Recall a significant product or career decision you made in the last year. Walk through it step by step: What options did you consider? What information did you have? What did you decide and why? The coach will help identify which cognitive biases may have influenced the process — not to judge the outcome, but to sharpen the process.
