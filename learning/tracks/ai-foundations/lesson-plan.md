# AI Foundations — Lesson Plan

**Updated:** 2026-01-01

## Current Status
New learner. No sessions completed yet. Begin with diagnostic to establish baseline.

## What to Cover First
1. **Transformers and attention** — Understand what the transformer architecture is, why attention matters, and how it differs from prior approaches (RNNs, LSTMs). Start with a concrete demo of what attention "looks at" in a sentence.
2. **Scaling laws** — Learn the empirical relationships between model size, data, compute, and performance. Understand why this matters for product decisions (cost, capability, timeline).
3. **Tokenization and embeddings** — Understand how text becomes numbers. What a token is, what an embedding captures, and why this representation matters for model behavior.

## Recommended First Exercise
Open any LLM (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) and give it a task that requires understanding context from far back in a conversation. Then give it one that requires recent context only. Observe the difference. The coach will use this to explain attention mechanisms — starting from what you can see, then building toward the mechanism.
