# World Models and Representation Learning — Lesson Plan

**Updated:** 2026-01-01

## Current Status
New learner. No sessions completed yet. Begin with diagnostic to establish baseline.

## What to Cover First
1. **What is a world model?** — Understand the idea that AI systems can build internal representations of the world that predict future states. Start with a concrete example: how a video prediction model "imagines" what comes next.
2. **Self-supervised learning** — Learn how models can learn useful representations without labeled data. Understand why this matters for scaling AI beyond curated datasets.
3. **JEPA architecture** — Understand the Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture at an intuitive level: why predict in representation space rather than pixel space, and what that buys you.

## Recommended First Exercise
Watch a 10-second video clip, then pause it. Predict what happens in the next 2 seconds — not pixel by pixel, but conceptually ("the ball bounces," "the person turns left"). This is exactly what a world model does. The coach will use your prediction to explain the difference between pixel-level prediction and abstract representation prediction.
