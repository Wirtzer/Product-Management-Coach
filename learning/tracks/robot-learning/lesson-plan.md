# Robot Learning — Lesson Plan

**Updated:** 2026-01-01

## Current Status
New learner. No sessions completed yet. Begin with diagnostic to establish baseline.

## What to Cover First
1. **Reinforcement learning basics** — Understand the agent-environment loop: states, actions, rewards, policies. Start with a concrete example like a robot arm learning to grasp objects through trial and error.
2. **Sim-to-real transfer** — Learn why training in simulation is attractive (cheap, fast, safe) and why transferring to real hardware is hard (the "reality gap"). Understand domain randomization as one solution.
3. **Manipulation vs. locomotion** — Understand the two major categories of robot learning tasks and why they present different challenges (contact physics, degrees of freedom, safety constraints).

## Recommended First Exercise
Watch a video of a robot learning a task (e.g., a robotic hand solving a Rubik's cube, or a quadruped learning to walk). Describe what you think the training process looked like: What was the reward? How many attempts did it take? Was it trained in simulation or on real hardware? The coach will use your intuitions to build toward formal RL concepts.

## Phase Progression
