# Example Coaching Session (Annotated)

This is a complete annotated example of a PM Coach session. It shows the flow from boot to track update.

> **Note:** This is a synthetic example for illustration. No personal data is included.

---

## Part 1: LP Stories — Storytelling Practice

The coach identifies this track is in the "Structuring" phase and selects the `basic-run-through` technique.

```
Coach: Let's work on your LP stories. You're in the Structuring phase,
so we're going to focus on getting the STAR format solid.

Pick one of your stories and tell it to me start to finish. I'll
listen and note where the structure needs work. Don't worry about
polish — just get it out.

Learner: Okay. So at my last company, we had this problem with delivery
costs. They were going up and nobody knew why. I looked into it and
found that one carrier was charging us more on suburban routes. I
talked to some people and figured out it was because of a contract
issue. We ended up optimizing the routes and saved a lot of money.
```

> **What's happening:** The coach used `basic-run-through` — a Storytelling Practice technique appropriate for the Structuring phase. The learner's response is a rough first draft.

```
Coach: Good raw material. Let me give you specific structural feedback:

📋 STAR Assessment:

**Situation** ⚠️
You jumped straight into the problem. I need context first:
- What company/team? What was your role?
- What were the stakes? ("going up" — by how much?)

**Task** ❌ Missing
What was YOUR specific responsibility? Not the team's — yours.

**Action** ⚠️ Vague
"I looked into it" and "I talked to some people" are too vague.
- What data did you pull? How did you segment it?
- Who exactly did you talk to? What did you learn?
- What options did you consider? Why did you pick route optimization?

**Result** ⚠️ No numbers
"Saved a lot of money" won't land. You need: "$X saved" or "Y% reduction."

Let's rebuild this. Start with just the Situation — give me 2-3
sentences that set the scene with specific context and stakes.
```

> **What's happening:** The coach scored each STAR element, gave specific actionable feedback, and used the `chunk-and-polish` approach — drilling one section at a time rather than asking for the whole story again.

```
Learner: I was a Senior PM on the logistics optimization team at
a mid-size e-commerce company. Over six months, our per-package
delivery cost had increased 18%, which translated to about $5M
in additional annual spending. Leadership asked my team to diagnose
and fix the root cause within one quarter.

Coach: Much better. 🎯

✅ Role is clear (Senior PM, logistics optimization)
✅ Stakes are quantified (18% increase, ~$5M)
✅ Time pressure established (one quarter)
✅ The "so what" is obvious — this is a big cost problem

Now give me the Task — one sentence about YOUR specific mandate.
```

> **What's happening:** The learner improved dramatically with focused practice. The coach reinforced what worked (specific praise, not generic "good job") and kept drilling.

After 15 minutes of practice on the full story:


## Part 2: PM Frameworks — Framework Application

The coach switches to the PM Frameworks track, which is in "Apply (Guided)" phase using the `guided-application` technique.

```
Coach: Now let's work on frameworks. You're in the Apply (Guided)
phase for CIRCLES, which means I'll walk through a case with you
and prompt at decision points.

Here's the prompt:

"How would you improve the experience of finding a restaurant
on Google Maps?"

Start with C — Comprehend. What questions would you ask to
clarify the situation before diving in?
```

> **What's happening:** The coach used a generic product sense prompt and applied the CIRCLES framework as a teaching vehicle. The `guided-application` technique means the coach walks alongside, prompting at each step rather than letting the learner go solo.

```
Learner: Okay. So Google Maps is a mature product, huge user base.
For finding restaurants specifically... I'd want to know: Are we
talking about finding a restaurant for tonight, or planning ahead?
Mobile or desktop? Is this about the search experience, the results
quality, or the decision-making process?

Coach: Good instincts. You correctly identified that "finding a
restaurant" is ambiguous. Let me push on one thing:

You asked "mobile or desktop?" — that's a reasonable clarification,
but what data might answer that FOR you without asking?

Learner: Usage data. Google Maps restaurant searches are probably
90%+ mobile. So I should just state that assumption and focus on
mobile.

Coach: Exactly. In an interview, stating "I'll assume mobile since
that's the dominant use case for this type of search — about 85-90%
of maps usage is mobile" shows product instinct. You're using data
to narrow scope, not asking the interviewer to narrow it for you.

Now move to I — Identify the Customer. Who are you designing for?
```

The session continues through the full CIRCLES framework, with the coach prompting at each step and providing feedback.

```
Coach: Nice work on CIRCLES. Here's your score update:

📊 PM Frameworks — Session Score:
  core_concepts:        45 → 52 (+7) ↑
  practical_application: 38 → 44 (+6) ↑
  current_research:     35 → 35 (—)  →

  Overall mastery:       42 → 46
  Quality rating:        3 (adequate)
  Next review:           3 days

You showed good instinct on Comprehend and Identify. The weak spot
was Cut (prioritization) — your criteria were vague. Next session,
we'll focus on making your prioritization criteria explicit and
quantifiable.
```
